Verb To Be
The verb To Be has 3 forms in the Present Simple - Am, Is, and Are.
They are used with the following Subject Pronouns :
I am / You are / He is / She is / It is
We are / They are

To form the negative of a sentence, all we have to do is add not.
For example:
I am not a doctor. I am a teacher.
She is not a teacher. She is a student.
You are not a student. You are a dentist.
Note the contractions:
I'm not... / You're not... / He's not...
She's not... / It's not... We're not...
They're not...

Also:
Jorge isn't a doctor.
Jack and Anna aren't good friends.
